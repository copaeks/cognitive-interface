Slide 2: Hyperphantasia – The Vivid Inner Canvas
================================================================================
Hyperphantasia is the ability to conjure hyper-realistic mental images, far beyond average visualization. For this mind, it's like having a built-in retinal projector: ideas appear in full color, depth, and motion, as if projected directly onto the "screen" of consciousness.

- Everyday Example: Imagining a recipe isn't just words—it's seeing ingredients floating in a kitchen, rotating them mentally to check textures, even "tasting" aromas through simulation.
- Tie to Innovation: This fuels the Cognitive AR Interface's retinal projection concept. Why? Because the mind already "sees" overlays on reality—AR just makes it external, accessible for collaboration in education, design, or gaming.

From our discussions: Inspired by Wreck-It Ralph, where worlds are spatial and interactive. Hyperphantasia turns code or workflows (like ComfyUI nodes) into floating graphs, editable in the mind's eye before they're built in AR.

Value from ASD: Neurodiversity amplifies this—ASD often enhances detail-oriented visualization, turning "daydreaming" into precise problem-solving. No shame; it's a lens that clarifies complexity.

Slide 3: Enhanced Proprioception – The Body in Space
================================================================================
Proprioception is the sense of where your body is in space, but here it's supercharged: an intuitive grasp of movement, balance, and positioning without visual cues. Combined with hyperphantasia, it creates a "mental physics engine"—simulating how objects (or ideas) move, collide, or interact.

- Core Insight: This mind can "feel" a gesture's trajectory before making it, or mentally "grab" and manipulate virtual objects with lifelike resistance.
- Real-World Application: In assembly tasks (like building a SpaceX motor), it simulates torque, weight, and fit internally—predicting outcomes before physical action. This inspired the ultrasound tracking in the interface: low-cost, passive rings that echo this internal body awareness.

From Brainstorming: Remember the "Modo Stark" vibes? Dragging holographic nodes feels real because the mind already assigns "weight" illusions—proprioception tricks the brain into perceiving virtual friction, reducing motion sickness as discussed.

ASD Context: ASD can heighten sensory integration, making proprioception a tool for mastery. It values ASD by showing how it enables intuitive spatial cognition, turning potential overwhelm into creative flow.

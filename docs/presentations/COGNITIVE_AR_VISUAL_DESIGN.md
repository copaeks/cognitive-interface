# Cognitive AR Interface Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

A vision of "mystical futurism," blending the esoteric with the technological. The aesthetic draws inspiration from the atmospheric density of Denis Villeneuve's films and the conceptual minimalism of brands like Nothing, creating a sense of profound, quiet innovation. The design philosophy is to visually manifest the project's core idea: externalizing the intangible mind into a tangible, spatial reality.

### Content Density

High Density. Each slide is designed to convey complex information, featuring 3-5 key points with a strong emphasis on data and technical diagrams. The layout prioritizes information clarity and depth, with a content-to-whitespace ratio of approximately 80/20 to support in-depth technical storytelling.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #0A0A0A (deep void black)
- Primary color: #2A2A2A (subtle charcoal)
- Secondary color: #555555 (medium gray)
- Accent color: #34D399 (emerald green - representing the acoustic shadow signal)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #0A0A0A
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #E5E5E5

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Chiron Hei HK (A modern geometric sans with sharp, precise lines, embodying a strong technological feel - used for main titles)
  - Hedvig Letters Sans (A distinctive sans-serif with unique character, ideal for creating a strong brand identity - used for special emphasis)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (A classic and highly readable sans-serif, ensuring clarity for dense technical information - primary body text)
  - Coda (A rounded and open sans-serif, offering a friendly and approachable tone for explanatory text - used for captions and annotations)

## 4. Image Style

Imagery should be atmospheric and conceptual. Favor abstract light paintings, out-of-focus bokeh in dark tones, and macro shots of technological textures. Images should evoke a sense of depth and the unseen, like visualizing sound waves or the void of space. Avoid literal stock photos of people using devices.
